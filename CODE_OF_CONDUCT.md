# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

This project exists to support nurses, student nurses, Digital Clinical Safety Officers,
and health IT developers. Everyone who participates must act with the same professionalism
and care we would expect in a clinical environment.

**Clinical accuracy is our highest priority.** Inaccurate clinical content can mislead
learners and ultimately affect patient safety.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviour

- **Be accurate.** Cite authoritative UK sources (BNF, NICE, NMC, NHS England, MHRA,
  Royal Colleges) when contributing clinical content
- **Be kind.** Treat all contributors — especially student nurses and those new to
  clinical safety — with patience and respect
- **Be constructive.** If something is wrong, explain clearly what the error is and
  provide the evidence for the correction
- **Be inclusive.** This resource serves internationally educated nurses (IENs) from
  many countries and cultures. Use clear, accessible language
- **Be professional.** Maintain the same standards of conduct you would in a clinical
  or educational setting

### Unacceptable Behaviour

- Publishing clinically inaccurate content without authoritative sources
- Presenting opinion as clinical fact or guidance
- Using NHS England, NMC, NICE, or MHRA logos or implying endorsement
- Reproducing copyrighted clinical standards text verbatim
- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary language of any kind
- Personal attacks, especially against contributors who report clinical errors
- Publishing private information without consent

---

## Clinical Content Disputes

Disagreements about clinical content are resolved by evidence, not by opinion:

1. The contributor who identifies the error cites their authoritative source
2. The maintainers verify against the original standard or guideline
3. The most current, authoritative UK source takes precedence
4. If ambiguity remains, the content is flagged with a review notice until resolved

**When in doubt, err on the side of patient safety.**

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests,
Discussions, and any other communication channels associated with this project.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour can be reported by opening a GitHub Issue or
contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be reviewed promptly.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.
Repeated or serious violations may result in a temporary or permanent ban from the project.
