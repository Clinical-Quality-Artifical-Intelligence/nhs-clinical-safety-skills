## Why These Languages?

These languages reflect the most common backgrounds of internationally educated nurses (IENs)
working in the NHS in England, based on NHS England workforce data:

- **Philippines** — consistently the largest single source of IENs in England
- **India** — second largest source; also covers Urdu-speaking nurses from Pakistan
- **Nigeria** — third largest source; Yoruba is the most widely spoken Nigerian language
- **Zimbabwe / East Africa** — significant source; Swahili and Shona cover this region
- **French** — covers nurses from Francophone West Africa (DRC, Cameroon, Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire) and Europe
- **Spanish** — growing number of nurses from Latin America and Spain
- **Arabic** — nurses from the Gulf, Egypt, Jordan, Lebanon, Morocco
- **Mandarin** — Chinese-speaking nurses and internationally trained doctors supporting nurses
- **Portuguese** — covers nurses from Brazil, Mozambique, Angola, and Cape Verde

## How to Verify a Translation

If you are a native speaker of any of these languages **and** a nurse or clinical professional:

1. Review the translation file for your language
2. Open a [Clinical Content Error issue](../../issues/new?template=clinical-content-error.md)
3. Provide the corrected term(s) with your professional background noted
4. We will credit you as a verifier in the file
