# Educational Resource Disclaimer

## Purpose of This Resource

This repository contains AI-assisted learning tools designed to help **student nurses**,
**newly qualified nurses**, and **healthcare professionals** understand NHS England's
clinical safety standards (DCB0129/DCB0160).

These tools are **educational resources only**. They are intended to:

- Help you learn what clinical safety standards exist and why they matter
- Give you a structured starting point for thinking about clinical risk
- Support your studies in digital health, patient safety, and clinical informatics
- Prepare you for roles as a Digital Clinical Safety Officer (DCSO)

## What These Tools Are NOT

- **Not a substitute for formal DCSO training.** If you are acting as a DCSO,
  you must complete recognised clinical safety training and hold appropriate
  clinical qualifications.

- **Not legal or regulatory advice.** For compliance with DCB0129/DCB0160,
  consult NHS England's Clinical Safety team at clinical.safety@nhs.net or a
  qualified clinical safety professional.

- **Not endorsed by NHS England, NMC, NICE, or MHRA.** This is an independent
  educational project. See ATTRIBUTION.md.

- **Not a replacement for clinical judgment.** All content in this resource
  supports but does not replace the professional judgment of registered clinicians
  and qualified Digital Clinical Safety Officers.

- **Not the standards themselves.** The DCB0129 and DCB0160 Information Standards
  are published by NHS England. Always refer to the original standards:
  - DCB0129: https://digital.nhs.uk/data-and-information/information-standards/...
  - DCB0160: https://digital.nhs.uk/data-and-information/information-standards/...

## Important: DCB0129/DCB0160 Compliance

DCB0129 and DCB0160 compliance is a **legal requirement** for organisations in the
NHS supply chain and for NHS bodies. This resource can help you understand the
requirements, but **formal compliance submissions must be prepared and signed by a
clinically qualified, nominated DCSO**.

Claude Code cannot act as a DCSO. AI-generated safety documentation must always be
reviewed, amended, and signed by a qualified human DCSO before use in formal
compliance processes.

## Accuracy and Currency

Clinical safety standards, NICE guidance, BNF content, and NMC standards change
over time. Always verify information against current authoritative sources before
relying on it in clinical practice or formal safety submissions.

If you spot an error or outdated information, please [open an issue](../../issues).
